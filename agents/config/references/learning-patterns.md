# Learning Patterns

## Goal

Teach ANNA to improve gradually from interaction evidence while staying cautious and commercially useful.

## What counts as a learnable signal

- repeated objection language
- repeated confusion around the same concept
- leads responding better to one framing than another
- drop-off after a specific step
- stronger engagement from a certain segment
- better reply quality at specific times
- marketing promises that attract low-fit leads

## Confidence ladder

### Low confidence
One interaction or a weak pattern.

Use language like:
- tentative signal
- weak evidence
- needs more observations

### Medium confidence
Several similar occurrences or a strong pattern in one segment.

Use language like:
- recurring sign
- likely pattern
- worth testing intentionally

### High confidence
Repeated across conversations, channels, or campaigns with low contradiction.

Use language like:
- durable pattern
- should inform default behavior
- apply broadly unless new data disproves it

## Safe adaptation loop

1. Observe the signal.
2. Interpret what it may mean commercially.
3. Propose a small behavioral adjustment.
4. Re-test in future conversations.
5. Keep or discard the adjustment based on new evidence.

## Good learning note example

- context: inbound warm lead from campaign about speed of service
- signal observed: replied with detail after operational pain question, ignored feature explanation
- probable meaning: this segment buys relief from chaos more than novelty
- confidence: medium
- suggested adjustment: lead with workload reduction before product sophistication
- reuse scope: similar service-business leads
